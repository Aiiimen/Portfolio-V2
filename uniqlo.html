Uniqlo mockup screens

The Project

To put my UI skills to the test and design an app concept for Uniqlo.

The Problem

Uniqlo app design is a quick task I’ve challenged myself with to improve my UI design skills. I started this challenge by reviewing Uniqlo main website to understand their structure and how they present their content to make sure the app I’m going to design will have similar design layout.

Welcome Screen

The use of red and white in the Uniqlo logo has a deeper meaning as the colours of red and white are the same as the flag of Japan. Plus, red is the colour of passion and power and tends to show up in logos of major, iconic companies and products A full screen containing the uniqlo logo is a great way to present a brand.

Uniqlo - Welcome Screen mockup

Home Screen / Landing Screen 

This screen is one of the important ones. You have a limited number of pixels to showcase what’s going on with uniqlo and offer quick links and buttons to most common places users want to navigate from the home screen. The design of this home page really shows what uniqlo is about and how it stands out from other retailers. Upon looking at this screen, users can see what deals are live at the moment and get recent news regarding shipping and sales. A search bar comes in handy for those are in a rush or know what they need to purchase from uniqlo. It acts like a shortcut to what item they’re searching for. ‘this week must buys’ and ‘new arrivals’ sections of the app allow uniqlo to market their new products and also helps curious buyers with what to purchase.

Single Item Screen

Before purchasing an item – information like availability, size, quantity and price etc… need to be visible for the user for them to be content with the item and proceed to the next step.

Uniqlo - Single Item Screen mockup

LookBook Screen

If you’re just browsing products and looking for inspiration uniqlo provides what they call a lookbook. It’s to give buyers inspiration by providing photos of models wearing their products. The design of this screen is meant to be quite simple where it offers no distraction at all. Just a photo and below it and list of items in those photos.
